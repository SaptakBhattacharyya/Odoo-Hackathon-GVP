# Frequently Asked Questions — FleetMetrics

## General

### What is FleetMetrics?
FleetMetrics is a fleet management platform that helps logistics companies manage vehicles, drivers, trips, expenses, and maintenance in one dashboard.

### Who can use FleetMetrics?
FleetMetrics supports four roles: Manager, Dispatcher, Safety Officer, and Finance. Each role has specific permissions tailored to their responsibilities.

### How do I get started?
Register for an account, then wait for a manager to approve your access. Once approved, you can log in and access the dashboard based on your assigned role.

---

## Vehicles

### How do I add a new vehicle?
Go to the Vehicles page and click "Add Vehicle." Fill in the license plate, make, model, year, type, capacity, fuel type, and other details.

### What vehicle types are supported?
FleetMetrics supports sedans, vans, trucks, and heavy trucks. Each type has different capacity and use-case considerations.

### How do I know when a vehicle needs maintenance?
Check the Maintenance page for open issues. Vehicles with status "In Shop" are currently undergoing maintenance. Critical severity issues mean the vehicle must be taken off the road immediately.

### What do vehicle statuses mean?
- Available: Ready for trips
- On Trip: Currently in use
- In Shop: Being repaired/serviced
- Retired: No longer active

---

## Drivers

### How are driver safety scores calculated?
Safety scores range from 0-100 and are based on driving behavior, accident history, complaints, and compliance records. A higher score means better safety performance.

### What happens when a driver is suspended?
Suspended drivers cannot be assigned to trips. A manager must review and lift the suspension before the driver can return to duty.

### How do I assign a driver to a trip?
When creating a trip, select an available driver (status: On Duty) from the driver dropdown. The system will show their license class and availability.

---

## Trips

### How do I create a trip?
Go to the Trips page, click "Create Trip," and fill in the origin, destination, cargo details, and select a driver and vehicle. The trip starts in "Draft" status.

### What is the trip lifecycle?
Draft → Dispatched → In Transit → Completed (or Cancelled). Each status change is timestamped for tracking.

### Can I cancel a trip?
Yes, you can cancel a trip at any stage before completion. You must provide a cancellation reason for record-keeping.

### How is fuel tracked?
Each trip records estimated fuel and actual fuel consumed. This data feeds into analytics for fuel optimization.

---

## Expenses

### How do I submit an expense?
Go to the Expenses page and create a new expense. Select the category (fuel, toll, parking, repair, misc), enter costs, and attach receipt if available.

### Who approves expenses?
Managers can approve or reject expenses. When rejected, a reason must be provided.

### What expense categories are available?
Fuel, toll, parking, repair, and miscellaneous. Each expense is linked to a specific trip, driver, and/or vehicle.

---

## Maintenance

### How do I report a maintenance issue?
Go to the Maintenance page, click "Add Issue," select the vehicle, describe the issue, set severity level, and assign to a workshop if applicable.

### What severity levels exist?
- Low: Minor, can wait
- Medium: Should be fixed soon
- High: Priority repair needed
- Critical: Vehicle must stop operating immediately

### How do I track repairs?
Each maintenance log shows the current status (open, in progress, resolved), scheduled date, technician, workshop, and cost.

---

## Analytics

### What metrics are available?
Fleet utilization, trip completion rates, fuel consumption trends, maintenance costs, driver performance rankings, expense breakdowns, and revenue per kilometer.

### Who can access analytics?
Primarily managers, but other roles can view analytics relevant to their responsibilities.

### How often is data updated?
Analytics are updated in real-time based on the latest data in the system.
